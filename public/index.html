Finding My Path

10.04.2020

You wake up, drag yourself out of bed and begrudgingly make your way to work for another day of surviving. We've all been there, right? That's how my life was until very recently.

Four months ago I decided to stand at the deep end and jump straight in. I am now a junior software developer at the end of a 12 week front end developer course with _nology, and I never had any idea that programming could be so awesome!

The idea that you can imagine something, and then write a long sequence of what used to look like an alien language so that you can literally give instructions to a machine to bring it to life is insane!

My work history is quite a varied one. I have had a variety of jobs, not really knowing exactly what I wanted to do.

I enjoyed learning some QBasic and writing text based games as a child and decided that I wanted to be a programmer when I grew up. However, I was always told I would need to study high level Mathematics to do so and that programming was boring, and so I drifted away from the idea.

My interest was sparked again last year and I decided to try and learn some C, which was challenging but enjoyable. Once I realised bootcamps existed, that you don't need a Maths degree and that I could actually pursue a career in development, I moved to Bristol to start a new chapter of my life and do the course, and I haven't looked back since.

I never imagined that I could enjoy something so much that putting endless hours of learning into it doesn't feel like work. I just wish there were more hours in the day so I could code more!

People always say to find work that you enjoy. Before I was merely working to live. I know that now I am back on the right path for me and am looking forward to bringing many ideas to life.

If you want to do something, don't be afraid. Dive in head first, or you may never get to see what lies under the surface.

Where Does Confidence End and Cockiness Begin?

14.05.2020

Best practices when applying for jobs.

Where Does Confidence End and Cockiness Begin?

14.05.2020

It's no secret that I've been on the hunt for work for the last few weeks since I finished the Software Development course with _nology and a few times, I've had to bite the proverbial lip.

I'll see a job i'm interested in and I can't help but to check their website out to see what they're packing... and what's this? On certain mobile resolutions the dropdown menu's extend outside of the box border and off the screen. On another, a text area has resizing disabled but the icon is still visible.

Ok, most people probably wouldn't notice things like this but to me they are things that need to be fixed. Then the dangerous thoughts start to pop up...

"Hmm, if I put it on my application the employer will clearly see that I have an eye for detail, and that I'm not afraid to be confident and speak up if something needs doing.", and as I consider the notion, the warning lights start spinning up and I consider it from another perspective.

Here I am, a Junior Developer applying for a job and having the audacity to tell a company on first contact what is wrong with their website? Probably not a great start.

Maybe I would get lucky and get through to someone who would appreciate the balls to the wall approach, but the chances of that are probably very limited, so I have adopted to remain passive... for the time being. I'm not afraid of speaking my mind but there is a time and a place.

In my opinion, the best approach for now is to show that you have attention to detail through quality work on your portfolio, and if you want to show confidence save it for the interview.

So... What do you think? I'd be interested to hear others people's opinions on the matter. Feel free to leave me a comment or send a message!
